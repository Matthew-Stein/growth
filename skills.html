In Time Enough for Love Robert Heinlein wrote1:

A human being should be able to change a diaper, plan an invasion, butcher a hog, conn a ship, design a building, write a sonnet, balance accounts, build a wall, set a bone, comfort the dying, take orders, give orders, cooperate, act alone, solve equations, analyse a new problem, pitch manure, program a computer, cook a tasty meal, fight efficiently, die gallantly. Specialization is for insects.

I am a big fan of this quote.

I am always taking on new skills, figuring out how things works, and trying to build my overall competance at a range of subjects. I don't really subscribe to the concept of mastery. Why would I want to dedicate myself to the relentless pursuit of perfecting one skill, when I could get to the 80-90% percentile 2 of a whole bunch of stuff?

I am also somewhat of a nerd; I love the large sprawling skill trees in RPGs. I wondered what making such a skill tree would look like populated with my own skills? How would I group things? How to visualize it? Seemed like fun!
